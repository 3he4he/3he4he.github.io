Research Interests

  • Emergent quantum states of matter
    • Strong electronic correlations, quantum phase transitions, non-Fermi liquid behavior and Kondo phenomena in d/f-electron materials
    Topological states of matter, topological Kondo insulators and semimetals
    Strong spin-orbit coupling limit
    Unconventional and high-temperature superconductivity
    Magnetism: interplay with superconductivity
    Charge-density-wave formation and influence to superconductivity
  • Tuning of physical properties via low temperatures, magnetic fields, pressure, chemical substitution
  • High-pressure physics
  • Search for new correlated materials: advanced material bulk crystal synthesis
  • Elastic and inelastic x-ray and neutron scattering

Technical Expertise

  • Low temperatures. Extensive experience measuring electrical transport, magnetic susceptibility, specific heat, thermal expansion and magnetostriction in dilution refrigerators (DR) at various institutions: SHE 3He-4He "Minifridge" dilution refrigerator with an 11 T superconducting magnet (UCSD), an Oxford Kelvinox MX100 3He-4He dilution refrigerator with a 9 T wide bore superconducting magnet for high pressure cells (UCSD); with an Oxford Kelvinox 400 3He-4He dilution refrigerator with a 20 T superconducting magnet (NHMFL - Los Alamos National Laboratory); with a Oxford Kelvinox MX100 3He-4He dilution refrigerator with a 14 T superconducting magnet (KIT). Custom-built pumped-4He refrigerator systems. Commercial PPMS and MPMS systems for electrical resistivity, specific heat and magnetization measurements.
  • High pressure. Extensive experience in the preparation of high pressure cells: diamond-anvil cells for elastic and inelastic x-ray scattering, and electrical resistivity measurements at pressures exceeding 100 GPa; hydrostatic clamped cells for electrical resistivity, ac-magnetic susceptibility and ac-calorimetry measurements at pressures up to 3 GPa; Bridgman-anvil cells, for electrical resistivity and ac-calorimetry measurements at pressures up to 16 GPa.
  • X-ray and Neutron Scattering. Installation, optimization and use of a 4-circle x-ray diffractometer (Huber) equipped with Cu and Mo tube sources and graphite and germanium monochromators, for temperature-dependent measurements (5-800 K) with a closed-cycle cryostat (ARS). Expertise in elastic and inelastic x-ray and neutron scattering experiments at various international user facilities: Triple-axis spectrometer (1T-TAS) at the ORPHÉE-reactor of the Laboratoire Léon Brillouin (LLB) at Saclay, France; double focusing triple-axis spectrometer (BT7) at the NIST Center for Neutron Research (NCNR) at Maryland, USA; inelastic x-ray beamline (ID-28) at the European Synchrotron Radiation Facility (ESRF) at Grenoble, France; inelastic x-ray beamline (Sector 30) at the Advance Photon Source (APS) at Argonne National Laboratory, USA. Experience with preparing and submitting proposals for experiments at user facilities.
  • Materials synthesis. Single- and poly-crystalline correlated electron material preparation: solid state reaction, molten-flux [filled skutterudites, CuFeTe2], Czochralski growth in multi-arc furnace (URu2Si2, UCoGe). Experience with glass bench (quartz/pyrex).
  • Materials characterization. Powder and single crystal x-ray diffraction in commercial diffractometer. Rietveld refinement for characterization of crystalline structure of materials. 4-wire electrical resistivity, magnetization measurements, torque magnetometry, Faraday magnetometry, ac susceptiptibility, heat capacity measurements in commercial (PPMS) and non-commercial equipment (DR). Thermal expansion and magnetostriction.
  • Computers and electronics. Vast experience in LabView programming for data acquisition systems.
