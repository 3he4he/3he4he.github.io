About me

I am an experimental condensed matter physicist, currently working as a University Assistant at the Institute of Solid State Physics of the Vienna University of Technology (TU Wien). My current research interests focus in quantum states of matter in strongly-correlated electron systems, in particular, topological Kondo insulators and Weyl semimetals. I study candidate materials using low temperatures, high pressures, and strong magnetic fields.

Before, I worked as a postdoctoral scientist at the Institute for Quantum Materials and Technologies (IQMT) of the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), in the New Materials and Thermodynamics group and in the Neutron and X-Ray Scattering group. My PhD work with Prof. M. Brian Maple at the Department of Physics of the University of California San Diego (UCSD) consisted in the study of the temperature-pressure phase diagram of strongly correlated electronic systems, where superconductivity, magnetism, and other electronic ground states coexist or compete. My research focused in the quasi-two-dimensional charge-density-wave rare-earth tritellurides (RTe3), and in iron-based superconductors. I followed this path since my undergraduate experience in high-pressures and low-temperatures at the Physics Department of the University of Buenos Aires, where I mainly studied the effects of uniaxial pressure on the dynamical properties of vortex lines in Bi2Sr2CaCu2O8+δ single crystals.

During my one-year stay at the National High Magnetic Field Laboratory (NHMFL) in Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL), I gained interest in quantum magnet materials, such as NiCl2-4SC(NH2)2, in which a Bose-Einstein condensate state can be induced by the application of magnetic fields, and acquired valuable experience in measuring specific heat in a dilution refrigerator at very high magnetic fields.

Main areas of research

  • Strongly correlated electron systems, quantum phase transitions, heavy fermion compounds, Kondo phenomena, unconventional superconductivity, charge density waves.
  • Correlation-driven topological insulators and Weyl semimetals.
  • Low temperature physics, high pressures, high magnetic fields, stress/strain dependences, single crystals synthesis and characterization, elastic and inelastic x-ray and neutron scattering.
